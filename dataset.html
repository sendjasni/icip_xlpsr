The Extreme License Plate Super-Resolution (XLPSR) Challenge

IEEE ICIP 2026 — Tampere, Finland

Benchmarking SR under severe, real-world degradations.

The IMPROVED Dataset

The XLPSR challenge is built upon the proprietary IMPROVED dataset (In-the-wild with multi-perspective realistic observations for vehicle evidence and license plate recognition), curated to capture the diversity and complexity of real operational environments.

Dataset Overview

  • Short real-world video clips (up to 10s) of moving vehicles with French license plates.
  • Multiple acquisition devices (consumer cameras, surveillance cameras, smartphones).
  • Wide range of distances (10–100 meters) and lighting (day, night, transitional).
  • Weather variation (clear, cloudy) and large viewpoint variability (frontal, oblique, steep angle).
  • Strong natural degradations (motion blur, sensor noise, compression).

Each video is paired with:

  • Low-resolution input frames \mathbf{I}_{LR} extracted from the video.
  • Ground-truth license plate text (character string).

No high-resolution clean frame is provided. The challenge evaluates text reconstruction accuracy rather than pixel-level fidelity.

Data Split and Training Policy

  • Training policy: Participants may use any external data (synthetic/public/proprietary). Only official test sets are restricted.
  • Public validation set: Representative subset with LR frames and ground-truth text for local validation and public leaderboard.
  • Private blind test set: Unseen videos with similar diversity; organizers run submissions to generate final CER scores.
