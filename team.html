Organizing Team

Meet the experts behind the XLPSR Challenge

An Official Grand Challenge at IEEE ICIP 2026

Tampere, Finland • 13–17 September 2026

Challenge Organizers

The XLPSR Challenge is organized by a team of researchers, engineers, and forensic experts from leading French academic institutions, industry, and the National Forensic Science Service.

CL

Chaker LARABI

Full Professor, Head of ASALI Research Group

XLIM Laboratory, Université de Poitiers

Chaker Larabi leads the ASALI research group at XLIM, focusing on image/video enhancement, perceptual optimization, quality assessment, compression, and immersive media. He has supervised over 20 PhD students, authored more than 200 publications, and led numerous national and international projects.

DN

Didier Nicholson

Research & Technology Director

Ektacom

Didier Nicholson specializes in image/video coding, transmission, and processing. With over 25 years of experience in standardization (JPEG/MPEG), he has supervised PhD students, served on 15+ thesis juries, authored 30+ publications, and led multiple research and technology transfer projects.

AS

Abderrezzaq Sendjasni

Postdoctoral Researcher

CNRS/XLIM, Université de Poitiers

Abderrezzaq Sendjasni specializes in computer vision and deep learning for visual media. He earned his PhD from Université de Poitiers and NTNU, focusing on perceptual quality assessment, super-resolution, and 360°/point cloud processing for enhanced visual experiences.

MV

Melan Vijayaratnam

Research Engineer

Ektacom

Melan Vijayaratnam works on AI applications for compressed video enhancement. He earned his PhD from Telecom Paris (IPP) with research on temporal extrapolation for zero-latency video transmission. His work has been presented at major conferences including ICIP, VCIP, and EUVIP.

GF

Gabriele Facciolo

Research Group Leader

Centre Borelli, École Normale Supérieure Paris‑Saclay

Gabriele Facciolo leads the image-processing group at Centre Borelli. His research focuses on mathematical modeling and machine learning for computer vision, image restoration, and remote sensing. He serves as Editor-in-Chief of IPOL and has led numerous research projects with both institutional and industry partners.
