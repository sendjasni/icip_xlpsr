Rules & Registration

Guidelines for participating in the XLPSR Challenge

An Official Grand Challenge at IEEE ICIP 2026

Tampere, Finland • 13–17 September 2026

Rules Under Development

The detailed rules and registration process for the XLPSR Challenge are currently being finalized. The information below provides an overview of what to expect. Complete rules will be published once approved by the IEEE ICIP 2026 Grand Challenge committee.

Please check back regularly for updates or follow announcements on the IEEE ICIP 2026 website.

Participation Overview

Eligibility
  • Open to researchers, students, and practitioners worldwide
  • Teams or individual participants allowed
  • Academic and industry participation welcomed
  • No participation fees required
Key Requirements
  • Registration through official challenge platform
  • Acceptance of dataset license agreement
  • Submission of 2-page extended abstract
  • Reproducible code submission (Docker preferred)
Important Note

While the complete rules are being finalized, we encourage interested participants to review the dataset details and challenge timeline to begin preparing their approaches.

Anticipated Rules & Guidelines

Based on similar Grand Challenges, we anticipate the following guidelines (subject to final approval):

Technical Requirements

  • Model Submission: Complete inference code with all dependencies
  • Reproducibility: Must run on standard GPU hardware without manual intervention
  • Documentation: Clear README with setup and execution instructions
  • Environment: Docker container or detailed environment specification

Submission Components

  • Super-resolution model outputs for test sequences
  • 2-page extended abstract in IEEE conference format
  • Source code with training/inference scripts
  • Pre-trained model weights
  • Validation results on public set

Prohibited Practices

  • Manual annotation or manipulation of test data
  • Using undisclosed external data
  • Multiple accounts or team splitting
  • Attempting to reverse-engineer the blind test set
  • Any form of cheating or unfair advantage

Permitted Practices

  • Use of any publicly available data for training
  • Pre-trained models and transfer learning
  • Data augmentation and synthetic data generation
  • Ensemble methods and model combination
  • External tools for preprocessing

Registration Process

1
Sign License Agreement

Accept the dataset terms of use and license agreement before accessing the data.

2
Complete Registration Form

All team members must provide accurate information through the official registration system.

3
Platform Access

Receive credentials for the challenge platform (CodaBench) and dataset download.

4
Begin Development

Access the development dataset and start building your super-resolution solution.

Registration Opening Soon

The official registration portal will open according to the challenge timeline. In the meantime, you can prepare by reviewing the dataset and forming your team.
