The Extreme License Plate Super-Resolution (XLPSR) Challenge

IEEE ICIP 2026 — Tampere, Finland

Benchmarking SR under severe, real-world degradations.

Overview

The Extreme In-The-Wild License Plate Super-Resolution (XLPSR) Challenge benchmarks reconstructing readable license plates from highly degraded video frames. Participants design a model \(\hat{\mathbf{I}}_{HR} = \mathcal{G}(\mathbf{I}_{LR})\) with magnification factors ranging from 8× to 16×. The focus is on real-world video frames with motion blur, compression artifacts, noise, and viewpoint variability. Models must recover character-level details without hallucination, ensuring reliability for downstream license plate recognition.

Frames may be processed independently or by exploiting temporal information across consecutive frames. Enhancement modules (denoising, deblurring, dehazing) are allowed.

Significance

Pushing SR into extreme regimes: Unlike common SR challenges (NTIRE, AIM), XLPSR addresses 8×–16× magnification under severe real-world degradations, motivating attention, diffusion-based SR, and degradation-aware pipelines.

High-impact application: Readable plates are critical for ITS, security, and forensics. The primary automated metric prioritizes functional correctness over purely perceptual quality.

Hallucination-free generation: OCR-based evaluation encourages fidelity and avoids plausible but incorrect characters, aligning with reliability in generative models.

Evaluation Protocol

Stage 1 — Automated Screening: An external OCR engine (fixed and disclosed) computes Character Error Rate (CER) as normalized Levenshtein distance; a Visual Quality Metric (VQM) such as BRISQUE/NIQE/MUSIQ is also computed. Combined score:

S = α · CER + β · VQM

The top submissions advance to Stage 2.

Stage 2 — Expert Evaluation: A panel evaluates visual realism, plausibility, conformity to official plate structure, geometry preservation, absence of hallucinations/artifacts, and overall fidelity. Winners are selected solely via expert evaluation.
